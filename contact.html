FAQ

Quick answers to common questions. If you don't see yours, email us—we're happy to help.

How soon can services start?

Most new clients start within 2–5 business days after the first conversation. Timelines depend on your location, schedule, and the type of support you’re looking for.

  • Urgent start: We’ll prioritize a safety check, a short plan, and begin with essential tasks while we arrange a regular schedule.
  • Planned start: We confirm your preferred days and times, match a caregiver, and send a simple confirmation by email.
  • Hospital discharge: We coordinate with family to make the transition home smooth (meals, pharmacy, mobility, etc.).

Tell us your ideal start date—we’ll be honest about availability and options.

Is there a minimum number of hours?

We’re flexible. Many families begin with a couple of visits per week and adjust over time.

  • Short visits: Great for check‑ins, companionship, or a quick errand.
  • Longer blocks: Helpful for personal care, meal prep, and appointments on the same day.
  • Trial period: Try a few visits first—you can increase or decrease later with no penalties.

We’ll recommend a minimum that fits your goals, but the final choice is always yours.

Can we change the schedule later?

Yes. Care plans are meant to evolve as needs change.

  • Simple changes: Email or call to move a visit or add time—we’ll confirm quickly.
  • Ongoing adjustments: We review the plan together (usually monthly) and update tasks or timing.
  • Back‑up support: If your usual caregiver is away, we’ll line up a trained backup and keep you informed.
Do you provide transportation to appointments?

Yes. We can accompany clients to doctor’s visits, community events, or errands.

  • Door‑to‑door support: getting ready, safe transfer, and on‑time arrival.
  • During the visit: respectful note‑taking (only if you wish) and helpful reminders afterward.
  • Afterward: a quick update to family so everyone knows how it went.

If driving is not appropriate, we can arrange taxis or rideshare and accompany the client throughout.

What types of tasks can a caregiver help with?

We tailor tasks to each person. Common supports include:

  • Daily living: bathing, dressing, grooming, toileting, mobility.
  • Home support: light housekeeping, laundry, tidy‑ups, meal planning & prep.
  • Health routines: medication reminders, gentle exercises, wellness check‑ins.
  • Community: shopping, social visits, faith services, walks, and hobbies.
  • Family respite: reliable coverage so caregivers can rest and recharge.
Are your staff background checked?

Absolutely. Safety and trust come first.

  • Reference checks and background screening before first shift.
  • CPR/First Aid and role‑appropriate training; PSW certification where required.
  • Ongoing coaching, shadowing, and spot‑checks for quality and consistency.

You’ll always know who is scheduled, and we welcome your feedback after each visit.

How do we pay?

We keep billing simple and transparent.

  • Clear hourly rates with no hidden fees.
  • Invoices by email; pay by Interac e‑Transfer or major credit card.
  • Receipts that you can use for insurance or reimbursement, if applicable.

If you have a budget in mind, tell us—we’ll tailor a plan that works.
